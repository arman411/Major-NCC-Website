Basic Drill Commands

सावधान (Savdhan)

Stand fully alert. Heels together at a 30-degree angle, chest out, chin up, thumbs along the trouser seam.

CO Vocal Cue: "Squad... Saav-dhan!"

विश्राम (Vishram)

At ease. Keep your left foot 12 inches away from the right foot. Lock hands behind your back, left hand over right hand.

CO Vocal Cue: "Squad... Vish-ram!"

सज दाहिने सज (Saj)

Dahiney Saj. Turn head to the right, take a 6-inch side-step, and raise left arm to adjust squad alignment and interval.

CO Vocal Cue: "Dahiney... Saj!"

दाहिने मुड़ (Dahiney Mud)

Turn 90 degrees to the right on the right heel and left toe. Keep body locked, step forward cleanly on count 2.

CO Vocal Cue: "Dahiney... Mud!"

बायें मुड़ (Baayein Mud)

Turn 90 degrees to the left on the left heel and right toe. Maintain Savdhan posture during the pivot.

CO Vocal Cue: "Baayein... Mud!"

कदम ताल (Kadam Taal)

March in place. Raise your knees to a 90-degree angle with the ground, pointing toes downwards on alternate steps.

CO Vocal Cue: "Kadam Taal... Taal!"

Syllabus & Exam Marks Structure

Certificate 'A' Exam

Taken by 1st-year Junior Division (JD) cadets. Passing requirement: 45% in each subject and 50% overall in written and practical combined.

Total Marks: 500 (350 Written / 150 Practical)

Certificate 'B' Exam

Taken by 2nd-year Senior Division (SD) cadets. Candidates must have completed at least 75% attendance and 1 training camp.

Total Marks: 500 (375 Written / 125 Practical)

Certificate 'C' Exam

The highest NCC certificate. Requires a valid 'B' Certificate, 3rd year standing, and attendance at 2 camps (e.g. CATC/TSC/RDC).

Total Marks: 500 (375 Written / 125 Practical)

Subject Topics Breakdown

1. Common Subjects (All Wings)
  • Drill Theory & Practice (80 Marks)
  • Weapon Training: .22 Deluxe Rifle (80 Marks)
  • National Integration & Awareness (30 Marks)
  • Disaster Management & Fire Safety (25 Marks)
  • Social Service & Community Dev (35 Marks)
2. Specialized Subjects (Wing-specific)
  • Army Wing: Map Reading, Field Craft/Battle Craft, Military History (110 Marks)
  • Naval Wing: Seamanship, Navigation, Ship Modeling, Semaphore (110 Marks)
  • Air Wing: Airmanship, Navigation, Aero-modeling, Airframes (110 Marks)

Emergency First Aid Protocols

🚨 CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

1
Check Response: Shake shoulders, shout "Are you okay?". Call for camp medical officer.
2
Chest Compressions: Place hands locked in the center of the chest. Push hard and fast (100-120 compressions/min).
3
Rescue Breaths: Open airway (tilt head back, lift chin) and give 2 breaths after every 30 compressions.

🐍 Wilderness Snake Bites

1
Immobilize: Keep the patient calm. Restrict movement and keep the bitten limb below heart level.
2
Clean & Bandage: Clean the bite area gently. Apply a broad pressure-immobilization bandage from digits up the limb.
3
CAUTION: Do NOT cut the bite area, apply ice, or attempt to suck out the venom. Seek immediate antivenom at TSC/RDC base.

☀️ Heat Stroke / Exhaustion

1
Cool Down: Move the cadet immediately to a shaded, well-ventilated area or ANO tent.
2
Hydrate: Give cool water or ORS rehydration solution slowly if they are conscious.
3
Lower Temp: Apply cool wet towels to the armpits, neck, and groin. Fan them continuously.

🦴 Fractures & Joint Sprains

1
Stabilize: Do not attempt to real align or push back bone ends. Keep the injured area completely still.
2
Splinting: Apply a rigid support (wooden ruler, branch, or rolled cardboard) secured with bandages above and below the fracture.
3
ICE Protocol: Apply ice pack wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling. Elevate if it is a simple sprain.
